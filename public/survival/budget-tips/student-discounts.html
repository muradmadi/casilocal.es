Shopping Guide

Student Discount Shopping in Madrid

Save Money on Everything

Madrid shopping doesn't have to drain your bank account. With the right student cards and insider knowledge, you can save 10-50% on almost everything from clothes to concert tickets. Here's how Madrid students shop smart without sacrificing style or quality.

Essential Student Discount Cards

Carné Joven (Young Person's Card)

⭐ Must Have
💰 €6/year 👤 Ages 14-30 🏠 Madrid residents

Where to Get It:

  • Community centers (Casa de la Juventud)
  • Online: carnejovenmadrid.es
  • Some universities issue them

What You Need:

  • ID or passport
  • Proof of age
  • Proof of Madrid residence (empadronamiento)

Major Discounts:

FNAC 20% off books, tech, entertainment
Decathlon 15% off sports gear
Telepizza 20% off
Clothing stores 10-30% off various
💡 Is it worth €6? Absolutely yes. One purchase pays for itself.

ISIC (International Student Identity Card)

🌍 Best for Travel
💰 €15-18/year 👤 Any enrolled student
150,000+ discounts worldwide Museum discounts Airline student fares Hostel discounts

Get it: isic.org

Carné Joven vs ISIC? Get both if you can. Carné Joven for daily Madrid life, ISIC for travel.

🎓 University Student ID (FREE)

Comes with enrollment. Often accepted for:

  • Museum entry discounts
  • Student nights at clubs
  • Some restaurant discounts
  • Campus gyms and facilities

Magic phrase: "¿Tienes descuento para estudiantes?" (Do you have student discount?)

Clothing & Fashion Shopping

Budget Fashion Stores

👕 Primark

Location: Gran Vía (huge flagship)

Prices: €3-20 for most items

Buy: Basics, seasonal trends, gym clothes

Skip: Anything you want to last years

👗 Zara, Pull&Bear, Bershka

Locations: Everywhere in Madrid

Sales: January & July = 50-70% off

Strategy: Shop sales only, buy basics at Primark

🏷️ Lefties (Inditex Outlet)

What: Zara/Bershka overstock

Prices: 30-50% cheaper than original

Best finds: Last season's Zara for Primark prices

🏃 Decathlon

Discount: 15% with Carné Joven

Running shoes: €20-40

Gym clothes: €5-15

Cheapest quality sports gear!

Secondhand & Vintage

Humana (Thrift Chain)

♻️ Best Value
📍 Malasaña, Fuencarral, others 💰 €3-15 most items

Best for: Vintage jeans, jackets, unique pieces. Sometimes extra student discounts (check in-store).

Flamingos Vintage Kilo

⚖️ Pay by Weight
📍 Malasaña 💰 €15-18/kg

Best for: Building vintage wardrobe cheap. Go when they restock (ask staff).

El Rastro (Sunday Flea Market)

🛍️ Sunday Tradition
📍 La Latina ⏰ Every Sunday morning
Vintage clothes Leather goods Jewelry Books

✅ Tips

  • Arrive early (9-10am) for best selection
  • Bargain expected (start at 50%)
  • Bring cash

⚠️ Watch Out

  • Watch your belongings
  • Pickpockets love crowds

Electronics & Tech

📚 FNAC 20% off with Carné Joven. Books, headphones, concert tickets.
🖥️ MediaMarkt No student discount. Shop Black Friday/summer sales. They price match!
🍎 Apple Education 10% for verified students through UNiDAYS.

Refurbished & Budget Tech

🔄 Where to Look

  • Cash Converters: Secondhand electronics
  • CeX: Games, phones, tech
  • Facebook Marketplace Madrid
  • Wallapop: Spanish eBay/Craigslist

⚠️ Safety Tips

  • Test before buying
  • Meet in public places
  • Check IMEI on phones
  • Get receipt if possible

Groceries & Food Shopping

Mercadona

🏆 Your Best Friend

No student discount BUT:

  • Cheapest base prices
  • House brand (Hacendado) = excellent quality
  • After 8pm: Red sticker discounts (50% off!)

Weekly Budget: €20-30

Pasta + sauce €2
Rotisserie chicken €4.95
Bread €0.50-1
Fruits & veg €5-7
Eggs €2

🛒 Lidl

Second cheapest option. Best for bakery, frozen foods, special weekly deals.

Bonus: Free WiFi!

🛒 Carrefour

Carrefour Club card (free) = regular discounts. Good for big shopping trips, variety.

Food Discount Apps

📱 Too Good To Go

  • Buy restaurant/bakery surplus
  • €3-4 for food worth €10-12
  • Pick up end of day
  • Reduces food waste

Best Madrid spots: Rodilla (sandwiches), various bakeries, some restaurants

Museums with Student Discounts

🖼️

Free: Mon-Sat 6-8pm, Sundays 5-7pm

🎨

Museo Reina Sofía

Student: €5 (Regular: €10)

Free: Mon & Wed-Sat 7-9pm, Sundays 1:30-7pm

🎬

Cinema

Día del Espectador: Wed or Mon = €5-7

Yelmo Cines: Student discount any day

⏰ Atrapalo.com Discount event tickets

Phone Plans & Gym

📱 Budget Phone Plans

  • Simyo: €10/month (20GB)
  • Pepephone: €9-15/month
  • Yoigo: Student deals sometimes

Pro tip: Get Spanish SIM immediately. Don't use roaming.

💪 Budget Gyms

  • McFit: €20/month, no contract
  • Basic Fit: €19.99/month
  • University gyms: €20-30/semester (cheapest!)

Free: Outdoor gyms, Retiro running, YouTube workouts

Seasonal Sales Calendar

❄️ January Sales (Rebajas)

Starts: Jan 7

Discount: Up to 70% off clothing

Best deals: Mid-January

☀️ July Sales (Rebajas de Verano)

Starts: July 1

Discount: Up to 70% off

Clear out summer inventory

🖤 Black Friday (Late Nov)

Electronics, tech, some fashion

Not as big in Spain as US but growing

📚 September (Back to School)

Stationery, electronics, clothes

University-area stores have deals

Shopping by Neighborhood

🏙️ Gran Vía Zara, H&M, Primark, FNAC. Busy but convenient.
🎨 Malasaña Vintage, thrift, independent boutiques. Best for secondhand.
🛍️ La Gavia Mall Everything in one place. Decathlon, MediaMarkt, Zara.
👟 Calle Fuencarral Pull&Bear, Urban Outfitters, streetwear. Higher prices, shop sales.

Your Student Shopping Checklist

📋 First Week in Madrid

  • ☐ Get Carné Joven (€6)
  • ☐ Download Too Good To Go
  • ☐ Join university Facebook buy/sell groups
  • ☐ Get Spanish SIM card (€10-20)
  • ☐ Find nearest Mercadona

❌ Don't

  • Buy full-price when sales happen twice yearly
  • Forget to ask for student discount
  • Shop at tourist areas (inflated prices)
  • Buy textbooks new without checking used
  • Pay full price for museum entry

✅ Do

  • Get Carné Joven on arrival
  • Shop Mercadona red sticker times
  • Use Too Good To Go app
  • Compare prices online first
  • Join university buy/sell groups
The Bottom Line: Smart shopping in Madrid isn't about having money—it's about knowing where and when to spend it. With these discounts, you'll live well on a student budget.
